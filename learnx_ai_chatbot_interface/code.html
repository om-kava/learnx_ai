Can you explain the basic principles of Quantum Entanglement and why it's considered "spooky action at a distance"?
Transmission Received • 12:44 PM
psychology

Quantum entanglement is a physical phenomenon that occurs when a pair or group of particles are generated, interact, or share spatial proximity.

Einstein famously called it "spooky action at a distance" because:

  • Instantaneous Interaction: Measuring one particle instantly determines the state of the other.

  • Locality Violation: Suggests state isn't limited by the speed of light.
